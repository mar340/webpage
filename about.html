About Me

Michael A. Rosario, PhD, is a cognitive neuroscientist and environmental health researcher originally from St. Croix, US Virgin Islands. His work focuses on how environmental conditions influence learning, memory, and the developing brain. He combines training in neuroscience, population health, and advanced statistical modeling to investigate the biological embedding of experience.

Dr. Rosario received his PhD in Neuroscience from Boston University and is currently a postdoctoral scholar at the University of Southern California. His research emphasizes the use of large-scale datasets and neuroimaging to examine brain development during childhood and adolescence.

He is committed to collaborative, interdisciplinary approaches that advance our understanding of human development across diverse environments.
